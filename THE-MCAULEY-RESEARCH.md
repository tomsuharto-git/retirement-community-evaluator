## DESCRIPTION

The McAuley is West Hartford's only Life Plan Community (CCRC), celebrating its 30th Anniversary of providing older adults with comprehensive retirement living and healthcare services. Located on a beautiful 26-acre campus adjacent to Saint Joseph College (now University of Saint Joseph), this Trinity Health Senior Communities facility has been voted Best in Independent Living by West Hartford Magazine for eight consecutive years. Partnering with Saint Mary Home as "The Mercy Community," The McAuley offers independent living apartments, assisted living, and access to rehabilitation and advanced nursing care, emphasizing creative pursuits, cultural engagement, and hospitality as core experiences.

## FINANCIAL PLANS & PRICING

### Payment Model: Life Plan Community / CCRC with Life Care Contract

The McAuley operates as a **Life Plan Community** (modern term for CCRC), offering Life Care contracts that provide long-term care coverage and security.

### Pricing Structure:

**Average Monthly Cost:**
- $5,369/month (average across all levels)
- Slightly above West Hartford's CCRC average of $5,250/month

**Pricing Components:**
- Entrance fee/buy-in: Likely required (Life Plan Community model), specific amounts not disclosed
- Monthly fees vary by:
  - Type of residence (studio, 1-bedroom, 2-bedroom, executive suite)
  - Level of care (independent, assisted, nursing)
  - Services selected

### Life Care Contract Benefits:
- Predictable costs for lifetime care
- Access to full continuum without major fee increases
- Priority access to rehabilitation and nursing care through Saint Mary Home
- Long-term care coverage built into contract

### What's Typically Included:
- Residence in independent or assisted living
- Dining services with multiple venues
- Activities and enrichment programming
- Transportation services
- Access to amenities (salon, fitness, wood shop, greenhouse, etc.)
- Priority access to healthcare continuum

**Note:** The McAuley did not disclose specific entrance fees or detailed pricing breakdowns in public sources. Prospective residents should request full financial disclosure including entrance fee ranges, monthly fees by apartment type, and Life Care contract terms.

## NOTES FOR SITE VISIT

**CRITICAL Questions to Ask:**
1. **Request full financial disclosure:**
   - Entrance fee amounts by apartment type
   - Refundability terms (declining scale, 90% refund, etc.)
   - Monthly fee structure by residence size
   - Fee increase history over past 10 years
   - What happens if assets are depleted
2. Tour wood shop, greenhouse, floral design space - gauge actual usage
3. Understand Life Care contract specifics vs. Fee-for-Service options
4. Ask about Saint Mary Home partnership - transfer process, timing, priority
5. Learn about University of Saint Joseph programming access
6. Review sample monthly activity calendar
7. Visit 19th hole golf lounge - who uses it, how often
8. Tour multiple apartment sizes including executive suites
9. Understand waiting list for independent living
10. Ask about resident demographics and community culture
11. Clarify pet policies in detail
12. Learn about transportation radius and schedule

**What to Observe:**
- Activity level in creative spaces (wood shop, greenhouse, floral, art)
- Resident engagement at dining venues
- Age/health profile of independent living residents
- Condition of 30-year-old buildings vs. renovations
- Staff interaction quality and ratios
- Dining quality and variety across venues
- Golf putting green usage
- Proximity to University and if residents actually access it
- Elizabeth Park accessibility from campus
- Overall hospitality feel (stated core philosophy)
- Evidence of 8-year "Best in IL" award quality

**Financial Transparency Concerns:**
The lack of published entrance fees and detailed pricing is a red flag for transparency. Be prepared to request comprehensive financial disclosure documents and compare carefully against other CCRCs.
