## DESCRIPTION

Avery Heights is Greater Hartford's premier continuing care retirement community (CCRC), nestled atop Cedar Mountain on a scenic 43-acre site with over five decades of service to the area. Owned by the not-for-profit Church Homes, Inc., Avery Heights uniquely offers a full continuum of care with NO buy-in fees—providing rental-only access to independent living in apartments and New England-style cottages, assisted living, memory care, rehabilitation, and skilled nursing. Developed in the 1950s by Hartford business executives and clergy, this established community features a state-of-the-art wellness center, diverse housing from cozy cottages to spacious apartments, and priority access to comprehensive healthcare services.

## FINANCIAL PLANS & PRICING

### Payment Model: Rental-Only CCRC (NO Buy-In Fees)

**CRITICAL DIFFERENTIATOR:** Unlike most CCRCs that require large entrance fees, Avery Heights operates on a **rental-only model with NO buy-in fees**. This makes it highly accessible for families who cannot afford or prefer not to invest $100,000+ upfront.

### Pricing Structure:

**Independent Living (The Heights):**
- Range: Estimated $3,000 - $6,000/month (based on apartment/cottage size)
- Apartments: One-bedroom and two-bedroom spacious units
- Cottages: New England-style individual homes
- Includes: Fully equipped kitchens, washer/dryer, cable hookups, private balconies/patios

**Assisted Living:**
- Starting at: $3,000/month
- Range: $3,000 - $7,000/month (depending on care needs)
- Includes: Personal care assistance, meals, activities

**Nursing Home/Skilled Nursing Care:**
- Starting at: $3,975/month
- Range: $3,975 - $12,927/month
- Average: $8,451/month
- Includes: 24/7 skilled nursing, rehabilitation services

### What's Included:
- Priority access to full continuum of care
- No entrance/buy-in fees required
- Rental flexibility (can move in/out without capital loss)
- Access to wellness center and all amenities
- Activities and programming
- Campus maintenance

### Financial Philosophy:
"With the flexibility of renting, residents gain priority access to a full continuum of care, including home health care, assisted living, memory care, short-term rehabilitation, and long-term skilled nursing care, ensuring they receive the support they need, when they need it."

## COMPARISON NOTES

**Compared to Other CT Communities:**

- **vs. Traditional CCRCs (The McAuley, Duncaster):** NO entrance fees vs. $100K-$500K+ buy-ins; rental flexibility vs. long-term commitment
- **vs. Assisted Living Only (Brookdale, AVIVA):** Offers FULL continuum including skilled nursing; more comprehensive healthcare access
- **vs. For-Profit Communities:** Not-for-profit Church Homes mission-driven model
- **MAJOR ADVANTAGE:** Only rental-only CCRC in research set—combines CCRC benefits with assisted living financial accessibility

**Strengths:**
- NO buy-in fees (game-changer for accessibility)
- Full CCRC continuum of care
- 43-acre campus (largest in research set)
- Over 50 years established (proven stability)
- Not-for-profit mission
- Cottage housing option (unique)
- Priority care access without entrance fees

**Considerations:**
- Skilled nursing costs can be high ($3,975-$12,927/month)
- Hartford location vs. West Hartford (less affluent area)
- Older facility (1950s) - may lack newest amenities
- Campus size could mean more walking/transportation needs

**Best For:**
- Families who cannot afford or oppose large entrance fees
- Those wanting CCRC continuum benefits without buy-in
- Nature lovers who value 43-acre campus
- Residents seeking cottage-style independent living
- People who prefer not-for-profit mission-driven care
- Those planning long-term aging in place with care transitions
